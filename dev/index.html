ERGMRank.jl

ERGMs for rank-order relational data: exponential-family random graph models for networks whose edge values are complete rankings, following Krivitsky & Butts (2017) and the R ergm.rank package.

The Model

Each ego rank-orders all alters. The observation for ego $i$ is a permutation of the $n-1$ alters, encoded as rank values with greater values indicating higher standing. The model is

\[P(\mathbf{Y} = \mathbf{y}) \propto \exp\left(\theta' g(\mathbf{y})\right)\]

on the space of complete orderings, under the discrete-uniform CompleteOrderReference — every valid rank configuration has equal baseline weight, so the structure comes from the sample-space constraint and the statistics $g$.

Highlights

  • RankNetwork enforces the complete-ranking invariant (each ego's ranks are a permutation of 1:(n-1)), preserved by swap_ranks! (the AlterSwap move)
  • Terms match ergm.rank exactly and are golden-master tested against R ergm.rank 4.1.2: RankDeference, RankNonconformity, RankNodeICov, RankInconsistency, RankEdgeCov
  • ergm_rank fits by swap-based maximum pseudo-likelihood
  • simulate_rank_ergm samples with AlterSwap Metropolis moves
